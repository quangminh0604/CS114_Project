Key Risk Factors

Understanding the major contributors to stroke risk

The likelihood of developing heart disease rises with age. Before menopause, men typically face a greater risk than women, but after menopause, women's risk increases to a level comparable to men's.

High blood pressure is one of the most critical risk factors for stroke. It puts extra strain on blood vessel walls, increasing the chance of rupture or blockage that can lead to a stroke.

Heart conditions such as atrial fibrillation, coronary artery disease, or previous heart attacks significantly elevate stroke risk by affecting blood flow or causing clots to form.

Smoking damages blood vessels, increases blood pressure, and reduces the amount of oxygen in your blood. Even light smoking significantly increases your heart disease risk.

Consistently high glucose levels may indicate diabetes or poor blood sugar control. Over time, this can damage blood vessels and nerves, raising the risk of stroke and cardiovascular disease.

Being overweight or obese can lead to conditions that increase your risk of heart disease, including high blood pressure, high cholesterol, and diabetes.
