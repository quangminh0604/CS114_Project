Recognizing Stroke Symptoms

Acting F.A.S.T. can save a life - know the warning signs of stroke

Stroke Emergency Warning

Stroke is a medical emergency! If you suspect someone is having a stroke, call emergency services (115) immediately. Every minute counts - quick treatment can save brain tissue and reduce long-term disability.

A stroke occurs when blood flow to part of the brain is blocked or when a blood vessel in the brain bursts. Brain cells begin to die within minutes, making immediate medical attention critical for the best possible outcome.

Remember F.A.S.T.

Use this simple test to quickly identify stroke symptoms:

F

Face Drooping

Ask the person to smile. Does one side of the face droop or feel numb?

A

Arm Weakness

Ask the person to raise both arms. Does one arm drift downward?

S

Speech Difficulty

Ask the person to repeat a simple phrase. Is speech slurred or strange?

T

Time to Call 115

If any of these signs are present, note the time and call emergency services immediately!

Common Symptoms of Stroke

Facial Drooping

Sudden numbness or weakness of the face, especially on one side. The person may be unable to smile normally or their face may appear uneven.

Arm or Leg Weakness

Sudden numbness or weakness in the arm, leg, or entire side of the body. May experience difficulty lifting or moving limbs.

Speech Problems

Sudden difficulty speaking or understanding speech. Words may be slurred, garbled, or the person may be unable to speak at all.

Vision Problems

Sudden loss of vision or visual disturbances in one or both eyes. May see double or have partial vision loss.

Severe Headache

Sudden, severe headache with no known cause. Often described as "the worst headache of my life."

Dizziness & Balance

Sudden dizziness, loss of balance, coordination, or sudden falls without explanation.

Confusion

Sudden confusion, trouble understanding, or difficulty with mental function and awareness.

Trouble Walking

Sudden difficulty walking, stumbling, or loss of coordination and balance.

Types of Stroke

Ischemic Stroke (87%)

Caused by a blocked artery in the brain, usually due to a blood clot. This is the most common type of stroke.

Hemorrhagic Stroke (13%)

Caused by a blood vessel bursting in the brain. Often accompanied by severe headache and has more sudden, severe onset.

Transient Ischemic Attack (TIA)

Also called a "mini stroke" - a temporary blockage of blood flow to the brain. Symptoms are similar to stroke but typically last only minutes to hours. Don't ignore a TIA as it's often a warning sign that a major stroke may occur soon.

When to Seek Emergency Care

Contact emergency services immediately if you or someone else experiences any of the following:

  • Any sudden onset of face drooping, arm weakness, or speech difficulty
  • Sudden severe headache unlike any experienced before
  • Sudden loss of vision or visual disturbances
  • Sudden dizziness, loss of balance, or coordination
  • Sudden numbness or tingling, especially on one side
  • Sudden confusion or difficulty understanding
Call 115 immediately if:
  • Any sudden onset of stroke symptoms, even if they seem to improve
  • Any combination of face drooping, arm weakness, or speech difficulty
  • Sudden severe headache unlike any experienced before
  • Sudden loss of consciousness or significant confusion

Remember: Even if symptoms seem to go away, still seek emergency care immediately. Time is brain!
